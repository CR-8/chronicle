# Getting Started with Combat Robotics

Welcome to the exciting world of combat robotics! This section covers everything from building your first combat robot to advanced battle strategies, weapon systems, and competition tactics.

## ‚öîÔ∏è What is Combat Robotics?

Combat robotics involves designing, building, and battling robots in organized competitions. Robots compete in arenas using various weapons and strategies to disable opponents while protecting themselves.

### Competition Categories
- **Antweight** (150g): Fast, agile robots
- **Beetleweight** (3lbs): Entry-level combat
- **Hobbyweight** (12lbs): Popular category
- **Heavyweight** (120lbs+): Powerful machines

## üõ†Ô∏è Combat Robot Building Guide

<Steps>
<Step>
**Choose Your Category**

Start with smaller robots to learn fundamentals:
- **Beetleweight (3lbs)**: Great for beginners
- **Hobbyweight (12lbs)**: Balanced challenge
- **Avoid heavyweights** until experienced
</Step>

<Step>
**Design Your Robot**

Consider these key factors:
- **Weapon System**: Spinner, flipper, or wedge
- **Drive System**: 2WD, 4WD, or omnidirectional
- **Armor**: Lexan or aluminum construction
- **Power System**: LiPo batteries with protection
</Step>

<Step>
**Gather Components**

Essential parts list:
- Chassis materials (Lexan/aluminum)
- Motors and speed controllers
- Weapon motor and mechanism
- Radio control system
- Batteries and charging system
- Safety equipment
</Step>

<Step>
**Build and Test**

Assembly process:
1. Build chassis frame
2. Install drive system
3. Add weapon mechanism
4. Wire electronics
5. Program and tune
6. Safety test thoroughly
</Step>
</Steps>

## üéØ Weapon Systems

### Beginner Weapons

<Tabs items={['Wedge', 'Spinner', 'Flipper']}>
<Tab>
**Wedge Bots**

**Pros:**
- Simple to build
- Very durable
- Good for beginners
- Low maintenance

**Cons:**
- Limited offensive capability
- Relies on pushing power
- Can get stuck

**Best For:** Learning robot building, pushing matches
</Tab>

<Tab>
**Spinner Bots**

**Pros:**
- High damage potential
- Exciting to watch
- Good for crowd appeal
- Can be very effective

**Cons:**
- Complex to build
- High maintenance
- Dangerous to work on
- Requires precise balancing

**Best For:** Aggressive players, high-risk strategies
</Tab>

<Tab>
**Flipper Bots**

**Pros:**
- Can flip opponents
- Good control
- Versatile strategies
- Fun to drive

**Cons:**
- Limited direct damage
- Requires good driving skill
- Can be countered

**Best For:** Technical drivers, strategic play
</Tab>

## üîß Essential Components

### Drive Systems
- **Motors**: High-torque brushed motors
- **Speed Controllers**: Hobby-grade ESCs
- **Wheels**: Aggressive tread patterns
- **Gearboxes**: Metal gears for durability

### Weapon Systems
- **Weapon Motor**: High-power brushless motors
- **Weapon ESC**: Heavy-duty speed controllers
- **Weapon Hub**: Precision machined components
- **Bearings**: High-quality, sealed bearings

### Electronics
- **Receiver**: 2.4GHz radio systems
- **Batteries**: LiPo packs with protection
- **Switches**: Heavy-duty power switches
- **Wiring**: Heavy gauge silicone wire

## üõ°Ô∏è Safety & Best Practices

<Callout type="warn" title="Combat Robotics Safety">

**Personal Safety:**
- Always wear eye protection
- Use hearing protection for loud events
- Wear gloves when handling sharp edges
- Know location of first aid stations

**Robot Safety:**
- Test all safety switches
- Use proper battery management
- Ensure weapons are secure
- Check all fasteners before competition

**Arena Safety:**
- Respect arena boundaries
- Never enter arena during matches
- Report damaged equipment immediately
- Follow all event rules and guidelines

</Callout>

## üéÆ Competition Categories
